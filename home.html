Smriti Garg

Director and Instructor

Hi, my name is Smriti Garg. As a child I always wondered how television, telephone, computers worked? How everyday things could be represented by a simple 0 and 1. This motivated me towards college and led me to study engineering. After completing my engineering (with a major in EE), I took my interest to the next level – designing microchips on VLSI (Very Large Scale Integrated Circuits) technology, ASICs (Application Specific Integrated Circuits) for various multinational companies like STMicroelectronics, Intel… for over 10 years.

Along the course, I developed a new passion – working with kids. Being a mother, organizing parties, theme based playdates, volunteering for school I had a lot of interaction with children. During these interactions I observed how kids have curious minds. They tried to get answers for every “Why” that came their way, thereby learning new science daily. This inspired me to start “Kewl Buddies Maker Labs” – A platform where children create, learn and build electronics gadgets while having fun every day.

Class Details

Types of classes being provided are: JUNIOR MAKER 101, MAKER 101 and MAKER 102

The pace of the class is designed for ages 7 to 9 …

This is the list of Lessons that the 8 week long Maker classes will focus on…

Kids will get introduced to Circuit, connection on Breadboard, Arduino and learn how to power it on and load a program.

Lesson 1 - Introduction:

Kids will get introduced to LED and build their first circuit on the breadboard.

Lesson 2 – Light Emitting Diode:

Children use LED, resistor and button switch in this lesson.

Lesson 3 -Circuit with input and output:

Kids build their own circuit to turn on and off an LED using ball switch.

Lesson 4 – Switch:

The intensity of an LED is changed here introducing the kids to the concept of different kind of signals.

Lesson 5 – Analog & Digital signal:

Children generate different colors on single LED which has 3 kinds of LEDs integrated together- the common anode RGB.

Lesson 6 – Multicolor LED Display:

Here kids switch on and off an LED lamp with presence/absence of light.

Lesson 7 – Light sensor:

Kids learn how we can switch on and off an LED lamp with varying loudness.

Lesson 8 – Sound sensor:

Here children build first complex circuit with multiple LEDs to give the effect of festive lights.

Lesson 9 – Festive lights:

Children switch on and off an LED lamp with a remote.

Lesson 10 – Infrared signal:

This lesson focuses on learning basic concepts of Circuit and Breadboard. Kids will get introduced to Arduino and learn how to power it on and load a program.

Lesson 1 - Introduction (maker 101):

This lesson introduces kids on how to build a circuit on a breadboard. Kids use an Arduino to control an LED and make it blink every second

Lesson 2 – LED Blink:

This lesson introduces the concept of switches to kids. Kids build their own circuit to turn on and off an LED.

Lesson 3 – Button Controlled LED:

In this lesson kids build a circuit and control the LED with a different kind of switch - Tilt Switch.

Lesson 4 – Tilt Switch:

This lesson focuses on the concept of Analog and Digital. Here we vary the brightness of an LED and introduce the kids to the concept of Pulse Width Modulation (PWM).

Lesson 5 – PWM Fading:

In this project kids build device to measure voltage of battery below 5 volts using resistors and LEDs. They make their first intermediate level circuit with more number of components.

Lesson 6 – Battery Tester:

Here we introduce the RGB (Red, Green, and Blue) LED to kids. Kids learn about a Color Truth Table and display various colors using an RGB LED.

Lesson 7 – RGB LED:

Using RGB, switches, LEDs kids. Build lamp which changes color based on the mood.

Lesson 8 – Mood Light:

Using couple of switches and resistors children build stopwatch, monitoring output on screen.

Lesson 9 – Stop Watch:

This lesson focuses on building an LED lamp that is controlled by Light. Here kids learn how we can switch on and off an LED lamp with presence/absence of light.

Lesson 10 – Light controlled LED lamp:

kids Using LEDs and LDR kids build project to show brightness in the room.

Lesson 11 – LED Bar Graph:

In this lesson kids learn how an LED lamp can be controlled by sound. They build their own circuit and understand how varying the sound can switch on the LED lamp.

Lesson 12 – Sound controlled LED lamp:

Here kids build and see the lamps changing intensity based on sound.

Lesson 13 – Musical Lamp:

This teaches the concept of Rolling LEDs. Rolling LEDs are similar to festive lights where one LED is on for a brief amount of time and then turns off. At the same time the 2nd LED turns on creating an effect of rolling lights.

Lesson 14 – Rolling Lights:

kids build project simulating oncoming traffic and how corresponding lights switch on.

Lesson 15 – Traffic Control Lights:

In this lesson Kids learn the concept of how remote works. They build a circuit and control an LED lamp with the remote.

Lesson 16 – Remote controlled LED lamp:

This introduces the concept of frequency using a buzzer. Kids create a circuit to sound a buzzer thereby learning more about transistors. Kids build Simon says game using Buzzer and other electronic components.

Lesson 1 – Buzzer:

This lesson introduces the concept of digital display to the kids using an eight segment display. They learn how to display different characters using the Arduino board. Using above they build fun Number Game.

Lesson 2 – Digital Display (1 bit):

This lesson introduces the concept of Potentiometer to the kids. This is also the first time Kids use the Analog controls provided in an Arduino board. Here children make Hit Zero Game.

Lesson 3 - Potentiometer:

This lesson extends the concept of lesson 2 and introduces a 4-digit display. Kids learn how “Dynamic Scanning Mechanism” works and use few digital pins to control 4 eight segment displays. 4 Digit Game is what kids build here.

Lesson 4 – Digital Display (4 bit):

In this lesson we build on the concept of Potentiometer and learn how a joystick works. Kids build a circuit and control a buzzer and an LED using a Joystick. Children Build Joystick Game; ping - pong here.

Lesson 5 - Joystick:

In this lesson we build circuit using 8*8 matrix Led display and display letter. Kids make their own piano here.

Lesson 6 – 8*8 Dot Matrix LED Display:

In this lesson we use infrared emitting diode on one board and control LED on another board. Laser Tag Is the game kids make here.

Lesson 7 – Infrared sender & receiver:

In this lesson we use LCD display. Children build their first video game - LCD game here.

Lesson 8 – 1602 LCD:
